                                                                                                                                                                Virgulilla in Spanish translates for tilde in English. In English, when the tilde is over a letter it is called a "combining tilde", otherwise it is a regular tilde.

                                                                                                                                                                In Spanish, the ~ symbol is called virgulilla, tilde de la ñ o virgulilla de la eñe. The term tilde in Spanish stands for both *any* ortographic accent (like á, é...) and the virgulilla (alone and over the n).

                                                                                                                                                                Yes, it is a bit confusing but it happens often when two languages describe the same reality in different degrees of detail... ;)
